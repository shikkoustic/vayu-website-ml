PM2.5

Particulate Matter < 2.5 µm

🔍 What is PM2.5?

PM2.5 refers to fine particulate matter with a diameter of 2.5 micrometers or smaller. These particles are so tiny that they can penetrate deep into your lungs and even enter your bloodstream.

To put this in perspective, PM2.5 particles are about 30 times smaller than the width of a human hair.

📍 Sources of PM2.5

Vehicle Emissions
Cars, trucks, and diesel vehicles produce fine exhaust particles during combustion.
Industrial Activities
Factories, power plants, and refineries release particulates through regular operations.
Construction Sites
Dust and debris from building and demolition activities enter the ambient air.
Burning
Combustion of wood, coal, crop stubble, and waste releases heavy particulate loads.
Natural Sources
Dust storms, wildfires, and volcanic eruptions contribute significantly to ambient PM2.5.
Indoor Sources
Cooking, smoking, and incense burning generate fine particles in enclosed spaces.

⚠️ Health Effects

Short-term
  • Eye, nose, and throat irritation
  • Coughing and difficulty breathing
  • Aggravation of asthma
  • Decreased lung function
Long-term
  • Chronic respiratory diseases
  • Heart disease and stroke
  • Lung cancer
  • Premature death

🛡️ Protection Measures

  • Wear Masks — N95 or N99 when AQI is high
  • Stay Indoors — Keep windows closed on polluted days
  • Air Purifiers — Install HEPA filters at home
  • Avoid Peak Hours — Limit outdoor activity during rush hours
  • Monitor AQI Daily — Check air quality before heading out
  • Indoor Plants — Keep air-purifying plants at home
