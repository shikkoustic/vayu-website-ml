NO₂

Nitrogen Dioxide

🔍 What is NO₂?

Nitrogen Dioxide (NO₂) is a reddish-brown gas with a sharp, harsh odor. It is one of several nitrogen oxides and plays a major role in the formation of smog and acid rain.

NO₂ is highly reactive and can cause significant inflammation of the airways.

📍 Sources of NO₂

Vehicle Exhaust
Cars, trucks, and buses are a major contributor of nitrogen dioxide emissions.
Power Plants
Coal and gas-fired power stations release NO₂ during electricity generation.
Industrial Processes
Chemical manufacturing and metal processing emit NO₂ as a byproduct.
Off-road Equipment
Construction machinery, ships, and aircraft contribute significantly.
Combustion
Burning fossil fuels for heating releases nitrogen oxides into the atmosphere.
Natural Sources
Lightning and bacterial activity in soil produce natural NO₂ emissions.

⚠️ Health Effects

Immediate Effects
  • Airway inflammation
  • Coughing and wheezing
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Increased asthma attacks
Long-term Effects
  • Development of asthma
  • Increased respiratory infections
  • Reduced lung function growth in children
  • Cardiovascular issues

🌍 Environmental Impact

  • Acid Rain — NO₂ contributes to acid rain formation
  • Smog Formation — Reacts with VOCs to form ground-level ozone
  • Water Pollution — Acid rain damages aquatic ecosystems
  • Visibility — Contributes to haze and reduced visibility

🛡️ Protection Measures

  • Reduce Car Use — Use public transport, carpool, or cycle
  • Stay Indoors — On high NO₂ days, limit outdoor activities
  • Avoid Traffic — Stay away from busy roads during peak hours
  • Ventilation — Ensure good air circulation in cooking areas
  • Air Purifiers — Use activated carbon filters
